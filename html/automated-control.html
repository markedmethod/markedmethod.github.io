Embedded Systems: Automation and Internet Connectivity


Mechanical Engineering provides the principles for proper design, constraint, anaylsis, and testing, but many times the implementation of these tools requires remote operation, continous monitoring, or many cycles of testing to prove a viable solution.

Example: how can one person assess the structural integrity of bridges across the country? Impossible unless some remote monitoring and continuous data analysis is performed to direct bridge inspections, and too cost-prohibitive to deploy hundreds of inspectors continuously.

Example: how can one person perform a complex sequence of mechanical loading patterns for fatigue lifecycle testing or vibration simulation of launch sequence events? Even the most complex assembly to produce these test characteristics would prove inadequate for some applications, and definitely costly to manufacture, procure, and maintain.

The solution to these class of problems is quite simple: use an embedded system or microcontroller to perform the most basic tasks required for the job. This allows distribution of skill without significant distribution of cost. Automation and improvement in system control allows me to work on other projects without getting bogged down.
